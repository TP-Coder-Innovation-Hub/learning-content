# Workflow: Research and Summarize with Critical Thinking

## What You'll Learn

- How to use AI for research without getting fooled by hallucinations
- Using Perplexity and AI search for real-time information
- Cross-referencing AI output with real sources
- The Discernment skill from the 4D Framework

## The Problem with AI Research

AI generates fluent text that sounds authoritative — even when wrong. This is called hallucination. It will confidently cite fake papers, invent statistics, and describe non-existent events.

Rules for AI research:
1. **Never trust a single AI output as fact**
2. **Always verify claims with real sources**
3. **Use AI to find and summarize, not to be your source of truth**

## Tool Selection for Research

| Task | Best Tool | Why |
|------|-----------|-----|
| Summarize a document you already have | Claude, ChatGPT | Paste the doc, ask for summary |
| Find current information | Perplexity, Google AI Overview | These cite real sources |
| Deep analysis of a topic | Claude Projects + your uploaded PDFs | AI reads YOUR sources |
| Quick fact check | Perplexity | Shows sources you can verify |

## Workflow: AI-Assisted Research

```
Step 1: Start with Perplexity for current information with sources
Step 2: Paste the sources into Claude for analysis and synthesis
Step 3: Verify key claims by clicking through to original sources
Step 4: AI drafts your summary or report based on verified information
```

## Exercise 1: Research a Competitor

Prompt for Perplexity:
```
What are the latest features and pricing of [competitor name] 
as of 2026? Include sources.
```

Perplexity returns answers with source links. Then paste into Claude:
```
Here are research notes about [competitor]. 
Summarize the key differences vs our product:

[Paste Perplexity output]

Format: Comparison table with columns: Feature, Them, Us
Then a 3-bullet executive summary of competitive advantages.
```

## Exercise 2: Summarize a Long Document

Paste a PDF or long article into Claude and ask:
```
Summarize this document. Include:
1. Main argument (2-3 sentences)
2. Key findings (bullet points)
3. Actionable takeaways for [your role]
4. Anything that seems unsupported or needs verification

Write in Thai.
```

The last point is critical — asking AI to flag uncertain claims trains your Discernment.

## Exercise 3: Create a Research Brief

```
I need a research brief on [topic]. 

Research approach:
1. Use Perplexity to find 5 recent sources
2. Paste all sources below
3. Have Claude analyze and synthesize

Requirements:
- Executive summary (100 words)
- Key findings with source attribution
- Contradictions between sources (if any)
- Recommended next steps

[Paste your sources here]
```

## Cross-Reference Checklist

Before using any AI research output, verify:

- [ ] Does the cited source actually exist? (Click the link)
- [ ] Does the source say what AI claims it says?
- [ ] Are statistics attributed to a specific study/report?
- [ ] Are there opposing viewpoints AI didn't mention?
- [ ] Is the information current enough for your purpose?

## Key Takeaway

AI is a research assistant, not a researcher. Use it to find, summarize, and synthesize — then verify everything yourself. The best research workflow combines AI speed with human judgment.
